About Me

I am an NSF GRFP Fellow and PhD Candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering under Dr. David Carlson at Duke University. My current work is focused on jointly modeling high and low-frequency signals (neuronal spike traces and local field potentials).

Education

  • PhD Electrical Engineering, Duke University (Anticipated 2026)
  • MS Electrical Engineering, Duke University (2023)
  • BS Applied Computational Mathematics, BYU (2020)

Experience

  • ETI Intern/Fellow, Oak Ridge National Laboratory (2022)
  • Data Engineering Intern, Capital One (2019, 2020)
  • Research Assistant, DRAGN Labs (2019-2020)
  • Research Assistant, Perception Control and Cognition Lab (2017-2020)

Research

Interests: Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence, Dynamical Systems Modeling, Hypothesis Generation
